What it does

One craft paves 16 blocks. Build BIG.

🛝

Channels snap like rails

Straights, corners, and slopes sort themselves out as you place them. Always full of water, no supports needed, dyeable in 16 colors.

🚀

Jets push any water

Each Jet drives a ~24-block current through the water ahead — slide blocks or plain water alike. Redstone switches them off.

⛰️

Uphill is a feature

A climb costs exactly what a drop pays, and jets re-pay the climb — so slides can loop, spiral, and go on effectively forever.

🫧

Tubes & drop shafts

Enclosed tubes commit you to the ride — window strips to see out, vertical shafts that bank speed for a screaming exit.

🏗️

Glass megastructures

Build a watertight glass tube of any size and the Flood Valve fills it — and if it isn't sealed, it points at the leak.

🛟

Safe by design

No fall damage while riding. Splash Pools swallow any landing. Crouch to brake, jump to bail — unless you're in a tube.

How it works

Three steps, splash included.

01

Lay the line

Slide Channels auto-connect as you place them — downhill, uphill, around corners. Gravity alone starts the ride.

02

Power the push

Place Jets along the run and feed them RF — the built-in coal- or water-fed Pump House, or any RF mod's generators.

03

Hop in

Crouch to brake, jump to bail, land in a Splash Pool. Mobs and dropped items ride too.

Rules of the water

Fun-first physics, honest limits.

~22 blocks/sec cap

Fast enough to feel reckless, tuned to stay under the server's movement ceiling. Configurable.

Symmetric slopes

Climbs cost what drops give, block for block. Endless loops aren't an exploit — they're the point.

Cheap power

One coal in the Pump House runs jets for a long while. Or skip it — any RF mod's cables plug straight in.

No chunk loading, ever

You load the world as you ride — a park can span a hundred chunks and only the parts near players tick. Mobs and items pause when you leave; that's by design.
