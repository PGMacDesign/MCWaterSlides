MC Waterslides — Design & Mechanics Spec

v1 planning doc · 2026-07-06 · PGMacDesign · status: agreed design (all numbers config-tunable and provisional until playtest)

1. Vision

A for-fun mod. Not endgame, not grind, not an economy to balance. Players build absolutely enormous waterslides — downhill, uphill, looping, effectively endless — and ride them fast, for joy or for transport. Cheap materials, RF-powered jets, and permission to break Minecraft's water physics in the name of fun.

Design pillars

  1. Fun first. Every mechanic earns its place by being fun, not by being balanced. No duplication-exploit paranoia (there is nothing here worth duping — see pillar 3).
  2. Build whatever you want. Free-form construction. No track validation, no "well-formed path" requirement. If you can shape water, you can ride it.
  3. Cheap everywhere. Copper + clay identity. No diamonds, no netherite, no rare drops anywhere in the mod. An iron pickaxe and a riverbank fund a full water park.
  4. Fast is the point. Top speed just shy of elytra flight. Slow lazy rivers are equally valid — speed is determined by how you build, not by tiers.

2. Core architecture: velocity-push riding

A rider in slide water is a normal entity the whole time — no seat entity, no mounting, no track lock. Slide blocks and jets apply motion to entities in their water. The rider can look around freely, bail out, wipe out, launch off an open ramp end, and splash into a pool. This preserves the water feel and keeps building fully free-form.

Rejected alternatives, for the record: seat-entity rides (roller-coaster mods) give smoother high-speed motion but feel like minecarts and force a track-network concept; a hybrid (auto-mount above a speed threshold) stays in the back pocket if playtests show top-speed jank.

3. The momentum model

Riders carry a managed slide momentum value (an entity attachment ticked by the mod), not raw vanilla velocity — vanilla water drag would cap speed near swimming pace. Momentum is a scalar speed along the current direction, integrated into actual movement each tick.

RuleBehavior
BuildJets add momentum while the rider is inside their current (see §4). Descending adds momentum per block dropped.
SpendAscending costs the same momentum per block climbed that descending grants (symmetric exchange). An upward-aimed jet re-pays the climb — this is what makes uphill and endless slides work.
BleedSmall per-tick drag. Momentum carries the rider through unpowered stretches until it runs out. Full speed coasts roughly 100 blocks in a channel.
CapHard cap ~22 blocks/sec (sprint ≈ 5.6, elytra cruise ≈ 30+). Config-tunable; the default deliberately stays under the server movement-check ceiling.

Feel targets: an 11-block drop from standstill hits the cap; a jet every ~20–24 blocks sustains cruise on flat; "lazy river" = sparse jets, "screamer" = dense jets + drops.

Rider controls & safety

  • Free look always. Slight steering influence in wide open water; none needed in 1-wide channels (the channel centers the rider through corners).
  • Crouch = brake. Jump = bail out (open channels/water only).
  • Closed tubes = committed. No bailing inside an enclosed tube until an opening — that's the thrill.
  • No fall damage while riding. After a bail or an open ramp-end launch, vanilla rules resume (water landings still save you). Splash Pool contact negates all landing damage.

4. The Jet: one physics primitive

The Jet is the mod's single physics driver: a block that projects a directional current through connected water.

  • Aim: placed facing any of the 6 directions — including straight up (geysers, vertical tube climbs).
  • Propagation: the current travels ~24 blocks (config) through connected water / slide-channel blocks in the facing direction, then dies; place the next jet to keep it going.
  • It energizes water itself, not just our blocks. Any water volume becomes rideable: build a hollow glass shell of any cross-section (2×2, 4×4, a cathedral), flood it, line it with jets — that's a closed slide. Channels/tubes are the precision-shaped convenience layer on top of the same mechanic.
  • Thrust: one fixed thrust stat (config). Speed control is jet count and spacing — how you build, not per-block GUIs. Redstone gates jets on/off.
  • Riders: players (fully polished), mobs (pushed like players; default on, config off — villager waterparks, pig races), dropped items (default on, config off — slides quietly double as item transport). Boats/minecarts: excluded from v1 (vehicle physics fights the current; a proper "slide raft" is a candidate v2 feature).

5. Power (RF)

Everything speaks the standard NeoForge energy capability — every RF mod's generators and cables just work, no hard deps (MC3DPrint's soft-compat philosophy). A standalone power kit ships so the mod works with zero tech mods installed.

BlockNumbers (defaults, config)
JetIdle ~4 RF/t while energized (keeps the current alive/animated); ~80 RF/t surge while actively accelerating a rider; 20,000 RF internal buffer so a flickering supply never stutters a ride mid-tube.
Pump HouseDual mode. Burning furnace fuel: 20 RF/t at a 5× burn multiplier — one coal = 8,000 ticks (~6.7 min) = 160,000 RF. Passive: ~2 RF/t while adjacent to flowing water (lazy-river trickle). ~100,000 RF buffer.
Water ConduitCheap waterproof cable, ~256 RF/t transfer, no storage.

Energy-parity constraint (deliberate): MC3DPrint's Clock Generator makes 10 RF/t at a 10× burn multiplier = 160,000 RF per coal. The Pump House produces the same total RF per coal, at double the rate for half the duration — more watts now, zero efficiency advantage. Neither generator supersedes the other when both mods are installed. Design target: one Pump House comfortably idles a ~5-jet slide.

6. Supporting mechanics

  • Flood Valve: RF-powered; fills the enclosed watertight volume it faces (bounded flood-fill, ~10 RF/block, volume cap config ~32,768) and drains it when toggled off. If the volume isn't sealed, it reports the leak location instead of filling — the "find the hole in my 400-block tube" debugging experience is a feature.
  • Splash Pool: catch block for slide exits — kills momentum smoothly within ~1 block, negates all landing damage on contact, pops the rider out standing.
  • Intrinsic water: channels and tubes are permanently waterlogged (cannot be dried) — real vanilla fluid rendering for free, no bucket ceremony.

8. Explicit non-goals (v1)

  • No economy/anti-exploit systems — nothing in this mod is worth exploiting.
  • No boats, rafts, or minecarts on slides.
  • No race timers/checkpoints (strong v2 candidate).
  • No website (mc3dprint.dev-style) at launch; Patchouli book is the onboarding surface.
  • No tiers, no upgrades, no progression gates.
